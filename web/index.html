Hi, my name is Dane Larsen. I'm a first year Master's student studying Computer Science at the University of Colorado (at) Boulder. I've been seriously programming for about six years now, with some brief experimentation before that with BASIC on a ZX81 that my Dad helped me build from a kit.

I'm good with C/C++, Python, Qt4, OpenGL/GLSL, OpenCL, Linux (Ubuntu primarily), Vim, Git, and lots of other odds and ends.

I am currently employed at the Laboratory for Atmospheric and Space Physics as a student programmer. I'm currently working on something called the MOC Clock, situational awareness tool for LASP satellite operators that displays countdowns to contacts and other interesting spacecraft events.

My proudest achievement so far has been contributing (in a very small way) to the Mars-bound satellite project, MAVEN.
